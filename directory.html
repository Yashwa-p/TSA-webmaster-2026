Nonprofits We Support

Newark Nurturing partners with organizations that bring creativity and compassion to life. Here are some of the wonderful nonprofits we collaborate with.

Epilepsy Foundation

"We directly serve people with epilepsy in Delaware with education, resources, self management programs, social events and more. We also train ANYONE who wants to be certified in Seizure First Aid for free."

BikeNewark

"BikeNewark seeks to move bicycling forward with its partner organizations and the city of Newark as a sustainable transportation and recreational activity which contributes to the quality of life in Newark, Delaware."

Pennie Make A Dime Inc. (PMAD)

"Pennies Make A Dime Inc. (PMAD) is committed to helping orphans and vulnerable children in Africa, especially those infected and affected with Burkitt Lymphomas, by enhancing access to health care, education, nutritional support and career development opportunities."

Newark Arts Alliance

"The Newark Arts Alliance provides the citizens of greater Newark with the opportunity to explore and display their creativity, as well as to participate in cultural events that build community. We do this by offering gallery space for exhibitions, an art gift shop, classes & workshops, regularly meeting art groups and special events. We are “inspiring creativity, innovation, and appreciation of the arts”."

>

Table of Plenty

"Table of Plenty provides emergency food and clothing assistance to families and individuals in the Millcreek/ Pike Creek area of Wilmington, DE"

The Newark Partnership

"The mission of the The Newark Partnership (TNP) will build upon Newark’s distinctive assets as an inclusive and innovative university community in which businesses, community institutions, city residents, and students work together toward the common goals of enriching the city’s prosperity and improving the quality of life."

Tender Hearts Inc

"Our mission to help medically fragile children have a better quality of life."

The B+ Andrew McDonough foundation

"The Andrew McDonough B+ Foundation honors Andrew’s life and his fight against childhood cancer. We raise funds through campus and community events, partnerships, and merchandise to provide support for families of children with cancer and to fund critical research. We are dedicated to helping kids fight cancer while spreading Andrew’s motto: ‘Be Positive.’"

Friendship House

"At Friendship House, we meet people in their most vulnerable moments with dignity, compassion, and unwavering support. Our programs are lifelines for individuals and families experiencing crisis, offering stability today and hope for tomorrow. Through comprehensive case management, financial assistance, a clothing bank and distribution services, life skills and job training, hospitality and supportive services, transitional housing, volunteer opportunities, and seasonal programming, we walk alongside our neighbors as they work toward self-sufficiency and long-term stability."

Friends of white clay creek state park

"We are a dedicated community of volunteers committed to protecting and enhancing White Clay Creek State Park, creating a thriving natural space for everyone to enjoy.”"

The trauma survivors foundation

"The Trauma Survivors Foundation is a nonprofit organization providing trauma-informed mental health support, crisis response, and professional training to individuals and communities impacted by traumatic events. With a focus on first responders and frontline professionals, TSF delivers licensed care and evidence-based education to strengthen long-term recovery and resilience."

Food bank of Delaware

"We distribute millions of pounds of food each year—everything from pantry staples to fresh produce from our own farms—while offering nutritional guidance to help families thrive. Whether it’s a mobile pantry in a low-income neighborhood, a school backpack filled with weekend meals, or a delivery to a senior’s doorstep, we meet our neighbors where they are with kindness and respect. But food distribution is just the beginning. To truly end hunger, we must tackle its root cause: poverty. The Food Bank equips our neighbors with the skills and resources to build lasting financial stability through innovative workforce training, financial coaching, and food benefits outreach."
